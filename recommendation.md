## 1. Strategic positioning and the differentiation bets to commit to

### The structural gap

The clipboard-manager market is mature but fragmented along four axes, and **no product wins on more than two at once** (`docs/competitive-analysis.md`, section 6):

1. **Cross-platform reach** (macOS + Windows + Linux, one mental model).
2. **Polish** (modern, native-feeling, fast, not a scripting console).
3. **Private sync** (E2E encrypted, works beyond the LAN, no vendor that can read or kill it).
4. **Approachability** (zero-config first run, depth revealed progressively).

This is the wedge. vbuff's job is to be the first tool that occupies all four corners simultaneously.

### Competitor four-corner table

Rated against the four wedge axes. ✅ strong, ◐ partial, ❌ weak/absent.

| Product | Cross-platform | Polish | Private sync | Approachable | The corner(s) it owns |
|---|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|---|
| **CopyQ** | ✅ Win/mac/Linux | ❌ dated, scripting-heavy | ❌ file-dir hack only | ❌ steep | Reach + power, nothing else |
| **Paste** | ❌ Apple-only | ✅ design-forward | ◐ iCloud, Apple-only, subscription | ✅ | Polish + Apple sync |
| **Ditto** | ❌ Windows-only | ❌ utilitarian | ◐ LAN P2P, plaintext DB | ◐ | Free Windows power, local-only |
| **CrossPaste** | ✅ Win/mac/Linux/Android/iOS | ◐ young | ◐ LAN-only, E2E | ◐ | Reach + privacy, no internet sync, unpolished |
| **Win+V** (built-in) | ❌ Windows-only | ◐ OS-native | ◐ text-only MS-cloud | ✅ zero-install | Frictionless default, shallow |
| **Maccy** | ❌ macOS-only | ✅ clean, search-first | ❌ none (local) | ✅ | Best free macOS standalone |
| **Raycast / Alfred** | ◐ mac (Raycast also Win) | ✅ | ❌ history excluded from sync | ✅ if you live in the launcher | Clipboard inside a launcher |
| **Espanso / TextExpander** | ✅ (Espanso) | ◐ | ❌ git/Dropbox or team cloud | ❌ (Espanso) | Text expansion, not history |
| **vbuff (target)** | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ E2E over internet, self-host | ✅ | **All four** |

### The bets to commit to (ranked)

**Bet 1 - Same tool, every desktop OS, identical behavior. (Highest leverage.)**
Ship one native-feeling, modern UI with identical keybindings and the same hot-path render code on macOS, Windows, Linux/X11, and Linux/Wayland. The architecture already enforces this: a single `egui`/`eframe` render path and four cross-platform traits (`ClipboardBackend`, `HotkeyBackend`, `PasteBackend`, `TrayBackend`) in `vbuff-platform`, with `vbuff-core` containing zero OS-specific and zero GUI code. **Vs rivals:** CopyQ has the reach but not the polish; Paste, Maccy, Pastebot, Raycast/Alfred have polish but are Apple-bound; Ditto is Windows-only. This corner is empty and it is the headline.

**Bet 2 - Zero-knowledge E2E sync that works over the internet, on by default, with a self-host/local-only option.**
Beat CrossPaste (LAN-only) and Paste (Apple-only iCloud) on reach; beat every vendor-cloud tool on privacy. Critically, **never depend on a single backend you can kill** - that is the Clipt lesson (shut down 2023) and the 1Clipboard lesson (abandoned beta on personal Google Drive). The architecture sequences this correctly: `vbuff-sync` with mDNS + Noise/TLS + SAS/QR pairing lands in v1 (LAN first), internet relay/cloud-drive transport in v2, payloads E2E so a relay sees only ciphertext.

**Bet 3 - Private by default, fail-closed, and provably so.**
Local-only out of the box, encrypted at rest (SQLCipher full-DB, key in the OS keychain), and a capture-decision gate that resolves every uncertainty to *skip* before any byte touches disk. The differentiator is not just doing this but *proving* it: the canary-grep at-rest test runs in CI on all three platforms and a hit is a release blocker. **Vs rivals:** Ditto stores a plaintext SQLite DB a corporate security team banned; Win+V, CopyQ (default), and Samsung Clipboard all keep plaintext history; OCR-on-everything tools (Clipory) turn copied receipts into searchable secrets. vbuff markets "your clips never leave your control" the way Ditto/CrossPaste do, but with cloud-grade convenience.

**Bet 4 - Power without a second app, and without a DSL.**
Fold snippets + text expansion (the Espanso/TextExpander job) and a transform pipeline (the Pastebot/CopyQ job) into the clipboard manager, exposed through a discoverable GUI with one-click actions, and later a scriptable CLI (`vbuff-cli`) and control socket for power users. **Vs rivals:** CopyQ gates power behind a JavaScript engine and dense config; Keyboard Maestro is Mac-only and $36; Espanso/TextExpander are separate tools. Casual users should never have to learn a scripting language to get one-click "paste as plain text" or "strip tracking params."

**Bet 5 - Trust and longevity by construction: open-source core, honest defaults, fair pricing.**
Open-source the core (the trust + longevity signal that Ditto, CopyQ, CrossPaste, Espanso all carry), zero telemetry by default, and monetize optional encrypted sync/teams with a one-time or fair-subscription model. **Vs rivals:** Paste and TextExpander both burned long-time users with perpetual-to-subscription bait-and-switch; ClipboardFusion sends opt-out telemetry from a tool sitting on your most sensitive data; CopyClip 2 shows ads inside a clipboard app. The abandonment graveyard (ClipX, 1Clipboard, Clipt, ClipMenu, Parcellite) means new entrants must actively signal they will still exist in five years.

### What we are deliberately NOT betting on (positioning non-goals)

- **Not a cloud product.** No vendor backend holds clipboard data by default (`architecture.md` non-goals). Relay/cloud-drive transport is opt-in v2 convenience, never the default.
- **Not an Electron/Tauri/webview app.** Synthetic keystroke injection, raw pasteboard access, secure-input detection, and sub-frame search redraw rule out a webview on the hot path.
- **Not a guarantee against a privileged local attacker.** Root/admin, an attached debugger, or a kernel-level attacker on the same box is out of scope. We defend against stolen disks, other unprivileged local users, shoulder-surfers, and on-the-wire interception.
- **Not a clipboard editor at capture time.** Transformations are a paste/quick-action concern; the stored canonical bytes are never mutated.
- **Mobile is a true peer, but later.** iOS + Android belong in the synced history as first-class peers (the gap Paste and the Windows tools leave open), but they are post-v2 work that depends on the sync layer being proven.

## 2. Build now / build later / skip matrix

"Build now" = the single-process MVP (crates `vbuff-types`, `vbuff-core`, `vbuff-store`, `vbuff-platform`, `vbuff-gui`, plus the root binary). The formal `vbuff-daemon`/`vbuff-ipc` split, `vbuff-sync`, and `vbuff-cli` are later phases. "Build later" = v1/v2. "Skip" = explicitly out of scope or a trap.

| Feature area | Verdict | Tier | Justification |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Capture: native event-driven backends** (Win `WM_CLIPBOARDUPDATE`, Wayland `wlr-data-control`, X11 XFIXES, macOS `changeCount` poll) | **Build now** | MVP | This is the product. `arboard` is the wrong tool (no events, single-flavor). Native per-OS capture behind `ClipboardBackend` is non-negotiable. |
| **Capture: all flavors byte-for-byte, dedup-by-hash, debounce** | **Build now** | MVP | Multi-flavor atomic capture + BLAKE3 dedup + move-to-top is core fidelity. Reading only `text/plain` is the #1 Linux complaint. |
| **Capture-decision gate (fail-closed)** | **Build now** | MVP | Concealed/transient hints, app exclusion, incognito, size cap, whitespace skip - every privacy promise routes through one ordered, testable gate before disk. |
| **Store: SQLite + SQLCipher, WAL, txn-per-capture, count cap, pins exempt** | **Build now** | MVP | Durability + encryption at rest + dedup is the floor. FTS5 schema present from day one even though substring is the shipped search tier. |
| **Store: out-of-row blob CAS, total-size cap, thumbnails, image transcode** | **Build later** | v1 | Image bloat (Ditto multi-GB DBs, Maccy memory leaks) is a real failure but the MVP count cap + per-item cap contains it; CAS + transcode is v1 hardening. |
| **Search: substring as-you-type + live highlight + type/pinned filters** | **Build now** | MVP | Keyboard-first recall is the core loop. egui `ScrollArea::show_rows` gives row virtualization for free. |
| **Search: FTS5 indexed, fuzzy, regex, relevance ranking** | **Build later** | v1 | Needed at 30k-50k items (CopyQ/Maccy collapse here), but the SQL infra is staged in MVP; switch the tier on in v1. |
| **Paste-back into prior focus** (CGEvent / SendInput / XTEST) | **Build now** | MVP | A picker that does not paste back is not a clipboard manager. Capture focus *first*, then write, then inject. |
| **Paste: plain-vs-rich, paste-stack, terminal-safe combos, restore-after-paste** | **Build later** | v1 | Plain-vs-rich one-shot is borderline-MVP; paste-stack/merge/FIFO are v1 depth. |
| **Snippets + text expansion** (abbreviation, folders, date/time placeholders) | **Build later** | v1 | The feature catalog tags a minimal set MVP, but expansion needs reliable secure-input handling and keystroke timing first. **Recommend deferring the abbreviation-expansion path out of the first shippable build** to keep the MVP focused on the copy->store->paste loop; saved snippets (pinned permanent clips) ship in MVP, expansion follows in v1. |
| **Transformations / quick actions** (case, trim, strip formatting, regex) | **Build later** | v1 | One-shot "paste as plain" is cheap and should be MVP-adjacent; the broader transform palette is v1. Never apply transforms to stored canonical bytes. |
| **Organization: pin, favorite, permanent, type filter** | **Build now** | MVP | Pinning is the most-wanted persistent-bank feature (Maccy, Win+V, Raycast). Pins must be a separate eviction-exempt class. |
| **Organization: tags, collections, named tabs, notes, drag-reorder, color-code** | **Build later** | v1/v2 | Depth that distinguishes a daily driver; not needed for first use. Pinboards/collections are a known Paste strength worth matching in v1. |
| **Tray / menu-bar item + autostart + single-instance guard** | **Build now** | MVP | The watcher must be live within a few hundred ms of login and survive popup dismissal. Even in the single-process MVP, the resident process + single-instance guard is required. |
| **Daemon/IPC formal split** (`vbuff-daemon`, `vbuff-ipc`) | **Build later** | v1 | The MVP runs watcher + store + GUI in one root-binary process. Promote to the formal daemon/IPC split when the CLI and external pickers need to reach the live watcher. |
| **CLI** (`vbuff-cli`: list/get/copy/add/search), external pickers, `vbuff://` | **Build later** | v1 | Scriptability is a core differentiator vs polished-but-closed rivals, but it depends on the IPC layer; sequence after the single-machine core is stable. |
| **Sync** (`vbuff-sync`: mDNS, LAN P2P, Noise/TLS, SAS/QR pairing) | **Build later** | v1 | Bet 2, but it depends on the entire single-machine store being proven. LAN first in v1, internet relay/cloud-drive in v2. The feature catalog tags some sync items MVP; that ordering is wrong and `architecture.md` correctly sequences them into v1. |
| **AI features** (semantic search, OCR, summarize/rewrite/translate, MCP server) | **Build later / mostly skip for now** | v1-future | OCR-on-everything is a privacy footgun (Clipory makes receipts searchable). On-device, opt-in, off-by-default only. The MCP-server-as-memory-layer is a future bet, not a near-term one. |
| **Collaboration / team** (shared snippet libraries, shareable links, roles, expiry) | **Build later** | v2 | The TextExpander niche. Lands last, after sync and conflict resolution are solid. |
| **Mobile (iOS/Android) as synced peers** | **Build later** | post-v2 | Real differentiator vs Apple-only Paste, but depends on the sync layer and platform share-sheet workarounds. |

### Four opinionated calls worth stating explicitly

1. **Pull abbreviation-based text expansion out of the first shippable MVP.** Saved/pinned permanent clips ship in MVP; live keystroke expansion (the Espanso job) is harder to get right than it looks (secure-input interaction, timing, word-boundary/case propagation) and risks the polish bar on the core loop. Ship it in v1 when the paste/keystroke infrastructure is hardened.
2. **The single-process MVP still needs a resident, supervised capture component.** Even before the formal `vbuff-daemon`/`vbuff-ipc` split, the root binary must hold the clipboard watcher, the single SQLite writer, the global hotkey, and the tray on background threads while the egui GUI runs on the main thread, guarded by a single-instance lock. "Capture only while a window is open" is a top competitor failure (CopyQ on Wayland/XWayland).
3. **Keep LAN/internet sync out of the MVP and say so loudly.** The wedge headline is "same tool everywhere," and sync is the second bet, but a half-working sync on top of an unstable store is worse than no sync. Stabilize the private single-machine core first; sequence sync as the first networked work in v1.
4. **Skip the in-MVP launcher/macro engine and the user-scripting DSL.** Do not become CopyQ. Power is delivered as discoverable GUI actions plus a later CLI, not a JavaScript/C# scripting console on the hot path.

## 3. Top competitor mistakes to avoid, by theme

Each row names the competitor(s) and maps to a concrete vbuff decision. Grounded in `docs/mistakes-top-500.md`, `docs/pain-points.md`, and the architecture's own "How vbuff avoids competitors' mistakes" table.

### Theme A - Silent data loss (the worst category)

| # | Mistake (who) | vbuff decision |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Clip lost when the source app/window closes** - the single most-reported Linux frustration (X11/Wayland core, GPaste, Klipper, Diodon, Clipman, CopyQ) | Eagerly materialize all offered MIME types into vbuff's own store the instant a selection event fires; on X11 take CLIPBOARD ownership and re-serve. vbuff *is* the persistence helper (built-in wl-clip-persist behavior). |
| 2 | **Capture only runs while the UI window is open / dies on XWayland window close** (CopyQ, cliphist, GPaste) | A supervised, always-on resident capture path decoupled from any window; a hidden persistent selection/message window keeps X11 capture alive. |
| 3 | **Missing rapid successive copies** (CopyClip, Maccy, Win+V, CopyQ) | Capture synchronously inside the event handler, enqueue, persist on a worker so the capture path never blocks and never debounces distinct edges. Zero-loss capture is a CI metric. |
| 4 | **History wiped or pins lost on app update** (Ditto silently emptied history on 3.21.134; Maccy lost entire history incl. pins) | The on-disk store is a version-independent contract: forward-only transactional migrations gated by `PRAGMA user_version`, automatic pre-migration backup, refuse to start rather than wipe on failed migration. |
| 5 | **Capture silently dies** - listener crash, broken viewer chain, GNOME Wayland unsupported, second manager fighting for the chain (Win+V service crashes, ClipboardFusion, CopyQ, Ditto) | Supervised capture with a heartbeat/watchdog that re-registers OS hooks (modern `AddClipboardFormatListener`, never the fragile `SetClipboardViewer` chain), plus an always-visible "capturing / paused / unsupported" health indicator so silent loss is impossible. |

### Theme B - Privacy and secret leakage

| # | Mistake (who) | vbuff decision |
|---|---|---|
| 6 | **Ignoring concealed/transient hints, storing passwords/2FA** (Maccy, CopyQ pre-fix, Ditto, GPaste, Flycut, Clipy) | Check every platform hint before storing - `org.nspasteboard.ConcealedType`/`TransientType`/`AutoGeneratedType`, Windows `ExcludeClipboardContentFromMonitorProcessing`/`CanIncludeInClipboardHistory`, KDE `x-kde-passwordManagerHint` - and skip flagged clips entirely. Zero bytes to disk. |
| 7 | **No default exclude list, so password managers leak** (Ditto, Flycut) | Ship a sane default deny-list (1Password, KeePassXC, Bitwarden) plus per-app include/exclude, applied before persistence. Defense in depth: hints + secure-field detection + app exclusion + secret detectors + regex + incognito. |
| 8 | **Plaintext, world-readable history; recoverable "deleted" secrets in freelist/WAL** (Ditto banned by a security team; CopyQ default; Win+V) | SQLCipher full-DB encryption, key in the OS keychain (never beside the DB); 0600/0700 files; `secure_delete` + WAL-truncate + `VACUUM` to scrub; canary-grep at-rest test on all three platforms as a release gate. |
| 9 | **OCR-on-everything indexes secrets** (Clipory makes a copied receipt fully searchable) | OCR/AI features are opt-in and off by default; sensitive items get masking, shorter retention, and sync exclusion. We do not index image text without explicit consent. |
| 10 | **Telemetry from a tool over your most sensitive data** (ClipboardFusion opt-out telemetry; CopyClip ads + phone-home) | Zero telemetry by default; open-source core so the claim is auditable; any future analytics is strictly opt-in. |

### Theme C - The paste action itself failing

| # | Mistake (who) | vbuff decision |
|---|---|---|
| 11 | **Auto-paste silently fails / inserts nothing / shows a notification of the text instead of pasting** (Maccy #1230/#1280, Ditto #245, ArsClip, ClipMenu) | Restore focus and confirm the target is frontmost before injecting; surface a clear message on detected failure instead of a silent no-op or a notification dump. |
| 12 | **Wrong character pasted / held modifiers leak** (Ditto injects a literal "v", #649) | Clear physical modifier state before injecting; use OS APIs (`CGEvent`/`SendInput`/`XTEST`) directly for ordering control rather than relying on a generic injector. |
| 13 | **Paste lands in the wrong window** (CopyQ on Wayland #2136, cliphist) | Capture the focus token first; on Wayland where injection is unavailable, set-and-let-user-paste rather than firing into the wrong target. Silent wrong-target is worse than a no-op. |
| 14 | **Popup steals focus and breaks the paste target** (Maccy #144, ClipMenu, Diodon) | Capture focus before showing the popup; the popup is a transient that restores focus on dismiss. |
| 15 | **Wrong formatting on paste; "paste without formatting" mangles content** (Maccy #109/#693, Alfred, Paste) | Store every co-offered representation atomically; decide plain-vs-rich at paste time; prefer `image/png` over an `<img>` HTML tag. |

### Theme D - Performance and resource collapse at scale

| # | Mistake (who) | vbuff decision |
|---|---|---|
| 16 | **Whole history rendered on every open; freezes at 30k-50k items** (Maccy #1097, CopyQ) | Virtualized, keyset-paged list (egui `show_rows`); never `SELECT *` the table. Sub-frame search-as-you-type at 100k+ items is a `criterion` benchmark gate. |
| 17 | **Unbounded SQLite growth; deleted rows never reclaimed -> multi-GB DBs** (Ditto reports of 14GB-216GB) | Hard count + total-byte ceilings with continuous eviction; `auto_vacuum=INCREMENTAL` + periodic `VACUUM`; current DB size shown in settings. |
| 18 | **Raw uncompressed Retina TIFFs (10-30 MB) bloat the DB and pin a core** (Ditto, Maccy, GPaste #40 loops re-adding a screenshot) | Transcode large images to compressed PNG; store oversized blobs externally by content hash with only a thumbnail in the row; refcounted GC. |
| 19 | **Memory leak / idle CPU spin / polling instead of events** (Maccy linear leak #384, ~45% idle CPU #993; CopyQ filesystem polling #1131) | Event-driven capture everywhere the OS allows; poll only the `changeCount` integer on macOS with adaptive idle backoff; near-0% idle CPU as a multi-day metric. |
| 20 | **Capture path blocks/OOMs on huge payloads** (CopyQ #3096: 700MB copy -> bad_alloc, monitor crash) | Stream bytes off the event thread with a configurable size cap; above the cap store a preview + external reference; survive allocation failure without killing capture. |

### Theme E - Linux/Wayland and platform-specific traps

| # | Mistake (who) | vbuff decision |
|---|---|---|
| 21 | **Relying on a protocol GNOME Mutter refuses to implement -> empty history on GNOME Wayland** (cliphist, clipman, wl-clipboard) | Probe compositor capability at startup; degrade to capture-on-summon + manual hotkey on GNOME Wayland; surface a per-compositor capability badge. Never fake capability (a stated non-goal). |
| 22 | **PRIMARY vs CLIPBOARD confusion floods or misses copies** (X11 core, GPaste, Klipper, CopyQ) | Treat PRIMARY and CLIPBOARD as distinct; capture CLIPBOARD by default; PRIMARY is opt-in and hard-debounced so mere selection does not flood history. |
| 23 | **Echo loops re-capturing our own restored/written clips** (GPaste, Clipy, CopyQ; one image pins a core) | Fingerprint (BLAKE3 + owner) every self-write with a short suppression window + sentinel format; on a self-write match, bump the existing row's timestamp at most, never insert. |
| 24 | **Routing the live DB through a cloud-sync folder -> `.conflict` files and corruption** (Ditto on Drive/Dropbox) | DB in a per-machine owner-only app-data path; sync at the record level through a real conflict-resolving protocol, never the raw SQLite file; warn at startup if the DB path is inside a known cloud folder. |
| 25 | **macOS Accessibility permission silently drops after updates and paste dies** (Maccy #159/#835, Flycut, Clipy); **login-item silently unchecks** (Flycut #206) | Detect `AXIsProcessTrusted()` false and fall back to copy-only with a banner that deep-links to the pane; verify autostart registration on launch; never assume a once-granted permission persists. |

Cross-cutting guarantee behind the whole table: the behavioral suite runs identically on macOS, Windows, Linux/X11, and Linux/Wayland; `vbuff-core` holds all dedup/fingerprint/retention/concealment policy as pure, fully unit-testable logic; and the resident process always exposes a visible capture-health state so the single worst failure - silent data loss - cannot happen unnoticed.

## 4. Differentiators worth pursuing (extras/gap sweep)

### Band 3 - Low / speculative impact, pursue only opportunistically or as future

These are real but niche; do not let them pull focus from the wedge.

- Application-aware auto-switching clip groups (#52), group-copy bundles (#55), named clipboards (#74) - power-user niche, v2 at most.
- AI summarize/rewrite/translate/captioning (#3, #4, #5, #11), on-copy math (#12), code explanation (#14) - v1-future, opt-in, on-device-first.
- Clipboard-as-MCP-memory-layer (#15, #111) - a genuinely novel future bet, but only after the core is a daily driver.

### Explicit traps to skip (or hard-gate)

| Trap (extras #) | Why skip / gate |
|---|---|
| **OCR-on-every-copied-image** (#2, #42, #101, #109) | Turns copied receipts/IDs/cards into searchable plaintext secrets (the Clipory failure). If built at all: opt-in, off by default, on-device only, excluded from sync. |
| **User-scriptable JS/C# transform engine with auto-triggers** (#37, #43, #108) | This is the CopyQ trap - power gated behind a DSL that casual users bounce off, and an arbitrary-code execution surface on the clipboard hot path. Deliver power as GUI actions + a typed CLI instead. |
| **Cloud sync via the user's own Google Drive / cloud-drive file** (#61) | The 1Clipboard/Ditto failure: `.conflict` files, corruption, and a dead backend. Record-level E2E sync, never raw-file-in-a-synced-folder. |
| **Web fallback / no-install pickup link** (#86), **background-capture entitlement abuse on iOS** (#94) | Either widens the trust surface (a relay that can see content) or fights the OS sandbox. Off the roadmap. |
| **Structured-data extraction + auto field-mapping on paste** (#18), **web-scraper clips** (#51), **list-to-checklist** (#13) | Scope creep into RPA/automation. Out of scope for a clipboard manager; revisit only if a clear user pull appears. |
| **Latest-clip injection into the mobile keyboard** (#91) | Platform-fragile, keyboard-extension-specific; future at best. |

## 5. Key risks and mitigations

Building on the architecture's risks table, with two feasibility spikes called out explicitly.

| Risk | Likelihood / Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| **egui native-feel is below the polish bar** the wedge demands. egui draws its own widgets and is the least native-feeling toolkit. Polish is Bet 1; if the popup looks "drawn," we lose to Paste/Maccy on their home turf. | Medium / High | **Feasibility spike (do this in Phase 0):** build the actual popup - virtualized list, type icons, thumbnails, match highlighting, dark/light, DPI scaling - in egui on all three OSes and judge it against Maccy/Paste side by side. egui wins decisively on the hot path (immediate-mode + free row virtualization); its weak spot is form-heavy settings and BiDi/complex-script shaping. Mitigate the latter with a `cosmic-text` galley for clip *content*. Keep `iced` documented as the escape hatch if native feel or complex-script fidelity is a launch blocker. Decide before the stack is locked. |
| **Internet sync feasibility** - the second bet is the hardest engineering item: NAT traversal, a relay that must be zero-knowledge, deterministic conflict resolution, and "never a backend we can kill." | Medium / High (v1-v2) | **Feasibility spike (before committing v1 scope):** prototype mDNS LAN pairing (SAS/QR) + Noise/TLS transport + a clip round-trip between two devices, and separately a minimal zero-knowledge relay proving it sees only ciphertext. Validate Lamport-clock + LWW conflict handling against concurrent edits and duplicate arrivals. LAN first in v1, relay/cloud-drive in v2. Build sync only after the single-machine store is proven stable. |
| **GNOME-Wayland has no sanctioned background clipboard monitor** (no wlr-data-control); a large Linux segment gets degraded capture. | High / High | Capability-probe at startup; degrade to capture-on-summon + manual hotkey; honest UI explainer and per-compositor capability badges. Document as a platform limitation, not a bug; track GNOME portal/extension progress. |
| **Wayland hides foreground-app identity** -> per-app exclusion and private-browser skip cannot function. | High / Medium | Make fail-closed-where-required configurable; show capability badges so users are not lulled into false safety; content/regex/secret rules still work and are surfaced as the active protection. |
| **Encryption silently not engaged** (wrong PRAGMA, fallback to plain SQLite, blob written before sealing). | Low / Critical | Canary-grep at-rest test on all three platforms in CI; pin cipher params in a config record; a canary hit is a release blocker. |
| **Sensitive content leaks** because a source app sets no concealed hint. | Medium / High | Defense in depth: hints + secure-field detection + app exclusion + secret detectors + regex + incognito; sensitive items masked, shorter retention, sync-excluded. |
| **Crate-maturity assumptions wrong** (`tray-icon` SNI, `global-hotkey` Wayland gap, `keyring` v3, SQLCipher PRAGMA defaults, objc2/windows-rs signatures). | Medium / Medium | Verify every platform crate against pinned versions in the Phase-0 spike before locking the stack; the trait boundaries make swapping an impl cheap. |
| **Self-write feedback loop** re-captures our own paste or a restored sensitive clip. | High if unhandled / Medium | Sentinel format + own-ownership tracking + short-TTL content-hash flag; debounce swallows the echo; gate #0 ignores tagged writes. |
| **DB corruption / power loss.** | Low / High | WAL + `synchronous=NORMAL`, txn-per-capture, `integrity_check` at startup, quarantine-and-restart on unrecoverable corruption, online-backup before migrations. |
| **Scope sprawl** across a 640-feature catalog + 122 extras. | High / Medium | Strict single-process MVP -> v1 -> v2 phasing with per-milestone exit criteria; sync/team features deliberately last so the private single-machine core ships and stabilizes first. Pull text expansion out of MVP (section 2). |
| **Single-machine MVP regresses on the daemon promise** - capture stops when the GUI closes, or two instances fight for the hotkey/DB. | Medium / High | Even in one process, run watcher/store/hotkey/tray on background threads with a single-instance guard; capture must outlive the popup. This is the bridge to the later formal `vbuff-daemon`/`vbuff-ipc` split. |
| **Trust/abandonment perception** for a new entrant in a graveyard-heavy market (ClipX, 1Clipboard, Clipt, ClipMenu). | Medium / Medium | Open-source core, public roadmap, zero telemetry, no subscription bait-and-switch, visible maintenance cadence. |

## 6. If we only do 10 things for the MVP (dependency-ordered)

Single-process build using `vbuff-types`, `vbuff-core`, `vbuff-store`, `vbuff-platform`, `vbuff-gui`, and the root binary. Each step unblocks the next.

1. **Workspace + types + trait skeleton + mock backends.** Stand up the Cargo workspace, `vbuff-types` (`Clip`/`Flavor`/`ContentKind`/ids), the four `vbuff-platform` traits, and `MockBackend`. Blocks everything; makes `vbuff-core` testable headless on any host.
2. **Encrypted store with txn-per-capture (`vbuff-store`).** SQLite + SQLCipher (full-DB, key in OS keychain), WAL, schema v1 + forward-only migrations, BLAKE3 content hash with a golden-vector test, FTS5 schema present (substring is the shipped tier). Depends on (1).
3. **Core engine: dedup, move-to-top, count-cap eviction, capture-decision gate (`vbuff-core`).** Pure logic over fakes; pins/permanent exempt from eviction; the fail-closed gate (paused/incognito/concealed/secure-input/excluded-app/size/whitespace). Depends on (1)+(2). This is the test crown jewel - proptest byte-fidelity + fail-closed invariants.
4. **One native capture backend, byte-for-byte, all flavors, debounced.** Pick the primary OS first; subscribe to native events (or poll `changeCount` on macOS), read every flavor in one pass, honor concealed/transient hints, dedup-by-hash. Depends on (1)-(3). This is the first end-to-end "copy lands in the store" moment.
5. **Resident process + single-instance guard + tray + autostart.** Watcher/store on background threads, global hotkey live within a few hundred ms of login, tray status item, single-instance lock with handoff. Depends on (4). Guarantees capture outlives the popup.
6. **egui popup with virtualized list + substring search-as-you-type + live highlight.** `ScrollArea::show_rows`, type icons, image thumbnails, empty/no-results states, pinned/favorite badges, full keyboard nav, dark/light, DPI. Depends on (5) and the polish spike from section 5. This is the face of Bet 1.
7. **Paste-back into prior focus.** Capture focus *first*, write flavors, dismiss popup, restore focus, inject the paste combo (CGEvent/SendInput/XTEST), with copy-only fallback where injection is unavailable. Enter / double-click / number-key. Depends on (6). Now it is actually a clipboard manager.
8. **Privacy floor: default app deny-list, incognito/pause, encrypt-at-rest verified, secure-delete.** Default-exclude known password managers, incognito flag, the canary-grep at-rest CI test green on the primary OS, `secure_delete` + WAL-truncate scrub. Depends on (3)+(2). Bet 3 made real.
9. **Organization basics: pin, favorite, promote-to-permanent, type filter, clear-all (pin-protected), delete-item.** Pins as a separate eviction-exempt class with stable order. Depends on (6). The persistent-bank feature every rival's users ask for.
10. **Cross-platform parity: the remaining capture/paste/hotkey/tray backends behind the same traits, on the per-OS CI matrix.** macOS, Windows, X11, Wayland-with-wlr-data-control; GNOME-Wayland degrades to capture-on-summon with a visible capability badge. Depends on (1)-(9). This is the exit gate: the wedge headline ("same tool everywhere") is only true once this passes on all four targets, with the canary-grep test green on all three OSes and cold-start hotkey live in a few hundred ms.

Everything beyond this list - FTS5 indexed/fuzzy/regex search, blob CAS + image transcode, snippets/text-expansion, the transform palette, the formal daemon/IPC split, the CLI, and all of sync - is v1+ and is sequenced in `plan.md` / `architecture.md`'s roadmap.

## Ideas and improvements backlog (product & strategy)

### Differentiation & positioning bets

114. **Provable-private mode with a live, auditable privacy ledger** `[M]` - Ship an in-app, tamper-evident log of every capture decision (captured / skipped-because-concealed / skipped-because-excluded-app / masked) plus an at-rest cipher-state readout, so users can SEE that secrets were never written rather than trusting a checkbox. _Value: Every rival asks you to trust an opt-out toggle; none can show you what it actually did. Turning the recommendation doc's fail-closed gate and canary-grep test into a user-facing, verifiable artifact converts vbuff's strongest internal guarantee into a marketable, screenshot-able trust wedge that Ditto (plaintext DB), Win+V, and CopyQ structurally cannot match._
115. **Format-fidelity contract with a round-trip self-test badge** `[M]` - For each clip, store every co-offered MIME flavor and run a continuous background round-trip check (capture -> store -> re-serve) that surfaces a green 'lossless' or amber 'degraded on this platform' badge per item and per app. _Value: The single most-reported cross-platform complaint is silent format loss (Linux text/plain-only, macOS 'paste without formatting mangles content', browser image-as-HTML). No competitor exposes fidelity as a measured, visible guarantee; making 'never silently downgrades your copy' a checkable claim is a defensible moat against CopyQ, cliphist, Maccy, and Alfred all at once._
116. **Identical-keymap guarantee across all four targets, enforced in CI and surfaced in-app** `[L]` - Define one canonical keymap and behavior spec, run the same behavioral test suite on macOS/Windows/X11/Wayland, and ship an in-app 'parity report' showing which actions behave identically everywhere (with native modifier rendering, not divergent bindings). _Value: The headline wedge ('same tool, every OS') is currently an aspiration, not a feature. Productizing parity as a tested, advertised guarantee is something no cross-platform rival (only CopyQ spans all three, with divergent UX) can claim, and it directly serves the macOS+Windows+Linux user who today carries three mental models._
117. **Backend-portable encrypted history vault (no vendor you can kill)** `[M]` - Define an open, documented, E2E-encrypted on-disk export/restore format that a user can park on ANY transport (a USB stick, their own S3/WebDAV, git, a NAS) and restore byte-identical on another machine, fully decoupled from vbuff's own relay. _Value: The graveyard (Clipt, 1Clipboard) burned users when a backend died; the docs name 'never a backend we can kill' as a core bet but stop at record-level sync. A specified, transport-agnostic encrypted container makes longevity a structural property a buyer can verify, neutralizing abandonment fear in a way Paste (iCloud-locked) and vendor-cloud tools cannot._
118. **Capability-honest UI: per-platform 'what works here' badges as a first-class surface** `[S]` - A persistent, glanceable panel that states exactly what vbuff can and cannot do on the current OS/compositor (e.g. 'GNOME Wayland: capture-on-summon only, no per-app exclusion, no auto-paste') instead of silently degrading. _Value: Silent capability gaps are the #1 source of Linux/Wayland frustration and false-safety (per-app exclusion that quietly does nothing). Every competitor either fakes capability or fails silently; being the tool that is loudly honest about limits is both a trust moat and a direct answer to the architecture doc's Wayland risks._
119. **Two-way live migration importers that adopt a rival's history AND keep it updated** `[M]` - Beyond one-shot import, run a continuous importer that ingests Ditto/CopyQ/Maccy/Win+V history in place so a user can trial vbuff alongside their current tool with zero loss before fully switching. _Value: Switching cost and abandonment fear are the biggest adoption barriers in this category. A 'run us in parallel, lose nothing, leave anytime' on-ramp lowers the risk of trying a new entrant to near zero, a defensible go-to-market wedge none of the incumbents offer (they assume you're already committed)._
120. **Deterministic, user-inspectable sync conflict resolution with a merge view** `[M]` - When the same logical clip is edited/pinned on two devices, surface a visible, explainable resolution (Lamport-clock + LWW shown to the user) with a one-click 'keep both' rather than the silent .conflict-file corruption that sank Ditto-on-Drive. _Value: Fragile, opaque sync is the universal complaint against Paste (iCloud), Win+V, ClipboardFusion, and 1Clipboard. 121. **Cross-device clip provenance and chain-of-custody** `[M]` - Stamp each clip with verifiable provenance (origin device, source app, copy time, which devices it has synced to) and let users filter and audit 'where has this clip been', with sensitive items flagged if they ever left a device. _Value: For privacy-conscious and team users, knowing a secret-bearing clip's travel history is genuinely novel; no clipboard manager tracks chain-of-custody. It compounds with E2E sync and the privacy ledger into a coherent 'private-by-default and accountable' story that vendor-cloud tools cannot tell._
122. **Reproducible-build + signed-history trust kit** `[L]` - Publish reproducible builds and let the app cryptographically attest that the running binary matches the audited open-source release, with a one-click verification, positioning vbuff as the clipboard manager you can prove isn't exfiltrating your clips. _Value: A clipboard manager sits on a user's most sensitive data; 'open source' alone (Ditto, CopyQ) doesn't prove the shipped binary matches the source. 123. **Self-destructing and 'view-then-burn' clips for secrets you must move once** `[M]` - A clip class that is captured, synced E2E to one target device, pasted exactly once, then provably wiped (secure-delete + WAL scrub) everywhere, for moving a one-time token or recovery code between your own machines. _Value: Today people paste secrets through Slack-to-self or plaintext history and they linger forever. 127. **Anti-exfiltration tripwire: detect and warn when another process scrapes the clipboard** `[L]` - Monitor for other processes repeatedly reading the system clipboard (clipboard-watching malware / second managers) and surface a clear alert, optionally auto-engaging incognito, turning vbuff into a clipboard-security sentinel. _Value: Clipboard-scraping malware and dueling managers (Maccy vs Pure Paste double-capture) are real, named threats with zero tooling today. ### AI / ML product opportunities

155. **Local embedding model as a hot-swappable platform trait** `[M]` - Define an EmbeddingBackend trait (alongside the four existing platform traits) so semantic features bind to a model interface, not a model, letting users pick bundled ONNX/GGUF small models, a no-op stub, or an external endpoint per device capability. _Value: Semantic search (#95) is already planned but undecided on the model; making it a trait keeps vbuff-core pure and testable with a mock embedder, lets low-RAM Linux boxes opt out cleanly, and avoids shipping one fat model that bloats the binary. Architectural enabler for every other AI idea here._
156. **Embeddings indexed at capture, stored in the encrypted CAS not a separate index** `[M]` - Compute the clip embedding inside the same txn-per-capture pipeline and persist the float vector as an encrypted CAS blob keyed by content_hash, so the vector inherits SQLCipher-at-rest and never lands in a plaintext side file. _Value: Most semantic-search add-ons leak a plaintext vector DB next to the encrypted store, silently breaking the privacy wedge. Reusing content_hash as the vector key means dedup automatically dedups embeddings too, and the canary-grep at-rest CI test already covers it._
157. **Privacy-tiered AI: per-clip 'AI eligibility' flag derived from the capture-decision gate** `[S]` - Extend the existing fail-closed capture gate to also emit an ai_allowed bit (concealed/secret-detected/excluded-app clips get ai_allowed=false), and have every embed/summarize/OCR path hard-refuse clips where the bit is false. _Value: Turns the recommendation's 'opt-in, off by default' AI stance into an enforceable invariant instead of a setting people forget. A copied password or JWT is structurally barred from any model, on-device or not, which is exactly the Clipory failure vbuff markets against._
158. **On-device intent classifier that routes clips to actions, not just tags** `[M]` - A tiny local classifier maps each clip to a likely next-action (open-as-URL, run-as-shell, paste-as-plain, decode-JWT, convert-color) and surfaces the top action as a one-keystroke default in the popup. _Value: Differentiates from rule-based auto-tag (#192/238) and from passive type detection (#170): it predicts what the user will DO, collapsing the transform palette into a single contextual suggestion. Fits the 'approachable, one-click actions' wedge (Bet 4) without a scripting DSL._
159. **Smart paste that adapts the clip to the destination app's expected format** `[M]` - Detect the focused target at paste time (already captured for paste-back) and apply a learned format policy, e.g. strip rich formatting into terminals, convert HTML to Markdown into editors, flatten to plain into chat, using a small on-device model over the existing per-app paste-rules. _Value: Per-app paste rules (#130/481) are manual; this learns them from the user's accept/undo signals so 'paste' just does the right thing per app. Highest-frequency daily action, and the focus token is already in hand from the paste-back design._
160. **Clip threading: group related clips into sessions via embedding + temporal clustering** `[L]` - Cluster consecutive clips that are semantically and temporally close into an inferred 'work session' (e.g. all clips while assembling one email), shown as a collapsible group in history. _Value: Solves the real pain of history-as-undifferentiated-firehose without manual tagging or folders. Reuses the embeddings already computed, plus the timestamp already stored. Novel: no catalogued feature groups by inferred task, only by type/tag/app._
161. **Natural-language history query over the local socket and CLI** `[M]` - Add `vbuff ask "the staging db connection string I copied last week"` that runs an on-device retrieval (FTS5 + embeddings) and returns ranked clips as JSON, exposed through the existing IPC/CLI client. _Value: Extends planned semantic search (#95) into the headless/automation surface the architecture already has (vbuff-cli, control socket), where power users and scripts live. Concrete vbuff fit: it is a pure IPC client verb, no GUI work, and dogfoods the same retrieval the popup uses._
162. **On-device PII/secret detector that augments redaction with model confidence** `[M]` - Combine the planned regex secret detectors (#336) with a small NER-style local model so it also catches free-form sensitive text (a pasted home address, a person's medical note) that regex misses, feeding the AI-eligibility flag and shorter retention. _Value: Regex catches structured secrets (cards, JWTs) but not unstructured PII, which is the larger real-world leak surface. Strengthens Bet 3 (private by default) and directly hardens what gets excluded from sync and from any AI path._
163. **Privacy-preserving sync of embeddings so semantic search works across devices** `[L]` - Replicate the encrypted embedding blob alongside each clip through the existing E2E vbuff-sync (relay sees only ciphertext), so a query on the laptop semantically finds a clip captured on the phone without recomputing on every device. _Value: Without this, semantic search is per-device and the phone (weak hardware) may never embed at all. The vector rides the zero-knowledge relay exactly like the clip payload, so it is the only multi-device AI approach that keeps the privacy wedge intact. Strong tie-in with Bet 2._
164. **Deduplicate by meaning, not just by bytes (near-duplicate collapse)** `[M]` - Beyond BLAKE3 exact-hash dedup, optionally collapse clips whose embeddings are within a cosine threshold (the same URL with a tracking param, the same paragraph re-copied after a one-word edit) into a single entry with variants. _Value: Exact-hash dedup (#40) misses the most common real clutter: trivially-different re-copies. Personalized and private by construction since the training set never leaves the device._

A security-flavored AI use that reinforces Bet 3 and is genuinely novel among clipboard managers._

### Roadmap sequencing & prioritization

185. **Attach hard kill-criteria to the two named spikes, not just 'do them in Phase 0'** `[S]` - For the egui-polish and internet-sync spikes the plan already mandates, pre-register numeric abort thresholds and an owner (e.g. 'if a blind side-by-side of the egui popup vs Maccy/Paste loses with >40% of testers, OR cosmic-text RTL clip content still mis-shapes, switch the documented iced escape hatch'; 'if a zero-knowledge relay round-trip can't be proven ciphertext-only in 2 weeks, cut internet sync to v2 LAN-only'). _Value: The recommendation.md spikes are framed as 'open questions a prototype can close' but have no decision rule, so a mediocre result will get rationalized into 'good enough' under deadline pressure. A written kill-criterion turns a spike into a real go/no-go gate and stops the polish bet (Bet 1) from quietly eroding._
186. **Pick the first capture backend by risk-retired, not by 'simplest to reason about'** `[S]` - M3 picks macOS first because changeCount polling is the easiest event model; instead build the highest-uncertainty backend first (Linux Wayland/wlr + the GNOME-Wayland degradation path), because that is where the architecture's own risk table rates likelihood AND impact 'High/High'. _Value: Sequencing the easy backend first front-loads confidence but back-loads the existential risk: if wlr-data-control + GNOME fallback turn out worse than assumed at M6, the whole 'same tool everywhere' wedge wobbles after the MVP is nearly built. Retiring the scariest platform at M3 means the four-corner headline is validated while it is still cheap to re-scope._
187. **Ship a throwaway tracer-bullet end-to-end loop BEFORE the rigorous M1 store** `[S]` - Insert an M0.5 that wires arboard + a trivial in-memory Vec + a raw egui list + global-hotkey into one ugly binary that does copy->list->paste-back on one OS, explicitly to be deleted, sequenced before the SQLCipher/migrations/CAS work of M1-M2. _Value: The current order builds the hardest-to-test substrate (crypto store, golden vectors, proptests) for 2-3 milestones before anyone sees a working paste-back. A disposable tracer bullet de-risks the integration seams (hotkey + focus capture + inject ordering) in days, surfaces the egui-feel question immediately for the spike, and gives a morale/demo artifact, without polluting the real crates the plan rejects arboard for._
188. **Build the format-fidelity corpus and round-trip harness before any real backend** `[M]` - Promote the UTI/CF_*/MIME format-mapping table and a checked-in corpus of pathological real clips (CF_HTML with BOM, RTFD, Excel OOXML clipboard, NSFilePromise, image/png-vs-img-tag, custom app MIME) to a standalone pre-M3 deliverable with a cross-OS round-trip harness, rather than table-driven tests living inside each backend. _Value: Multi-flavor byte-fidelity is the #1 Linux complaint and a stated MVP foundation, but the plan only tests it per-backend as each lands, so the same fidelity bug gets rediscovered four times and there's no shared oracle. A platform-agnostic corpus lets every backend prove conformance against one fixed target and catches the 'image arrives as HTML' class once._
189. **Pull packaging, signing and notarization left into a smoke-gate at M4, not M10** `[M]` - Add a minimal 'signed artifact installs on a clean VM' lane the moment the first OS loop works (M4) for that one OS, instead of deferring all of macOS notarization / Windows EV signing / Linux portal-permission packaging to M10. _Value: Signing/notarization/Gatekeeper/SmartScreen and Flatpak portal permissions are exactly the class of failure that 'blocked launch via signing gaps' warns about, and they routinely take weeks and block on certs/Apple review that can't be compressed. Discovering at M10 that the hardened runtime breaks Keychain ACL access, or that the Flatpak sandbox blocks the GlobalShortcuts portal, is a worst-case-timing surprise; a thin early smoke-gate de-risks it cheaply._
190. **Insert a 'Wayland reality-check' gate between M3 and committing to all four backends** `[S]` - After the first backend and conformance battery exist, run a focused 1-week probe on real GNOME-Wayland, KDE-Wayland and sway to confirm wlr-data-control availability, GlobalShortcuts-portal hotkeys, and virtual-keyboard paste injection actually work, with an explicit decision: full capture vs documented capture-on-summon vs drop a compositor from MVP scope. _Value: The plan commits M6 to Windows + X11 + wlr + GNOME-fallback as one block, but Wayland injection/hotkey/portal support varies wildly by compositor and version and is the highest-rated risk. A reality-check gate prevents M6 from ballooning into an open-ended compositor whack-a-mole and lets the MVP honestly scope which Linux sessions are first-class at launch._
191. **Split the monolithic M9 sync milestone into four independently-killable sub-spikes** `[M]` - Decompose 'LAN P2P sync' into ordered, separately-abortable pieces: (a) mDNS discovery + SAS/QR pairing, (b) Noise/TLS transport with a ciphertext-only packet-capture proof, (c) Lamport+LWW replication of plain text only, (d) typed/large-object sync via CAS, each with its own exit test. _Value: The plan calls M9 'the heaviest single milestone' yet treats it as one unit, so a stall in conflict resolution (the genuinely hard part) blocks the shippable parts (pairing, transport). Independent sub-spikes let the trust-building wins (E2E proven, devices pair) land and demo even if conflict-convergence or large-object sync slips to a later cut._
192. **Define explicit scope-trap tripwires that auto-trigger a re-scope review** `[S]` - Encode the 'scope sprawl across 640 features' risk as concrete tripwires checked at each milestone exit: e.g. 'MVP milestone count > planned by 1 => cut a feature, don't add one', 'any feature requiring a new crate beyond the six MVP crates is auto-deferred', 'any single milestone open >N weeks triggers a cut-line review'. _Value: Scope sprawl is rated High-likelihood in the risk table but mitigated only by the general phasing. Tripwires turn 'be disciplined' into an automatic forcing function, which is what actually keeps a 640-item catalog from leaking into the MVP one reasonable-sounding feature at a time._
193. **Sequence an internal-dogfood alpha at MVP-on-first-OS (end of M5), gated before M6 fan-out** `[S]` - Make 'maintainers run vbuff as their only clipboard manager on the first OS for 2 weeks' an explicit milestone between M5 and M6, with a bug-bar gate (zero silent-loss incidents, zero wrong-target pastes) that must pass before spending effort porting to three more platforms. _Value: The plan's gates are CI/benchmark gates; none is 'a human used it daily and trusted it.' Silent data loss, focus-theft, and echo loops are exactly the bugs CI under-detects but daily use surfaces in hours. Dogfooding on one OS before the 4x backend investment means platform parity is built on a loop that's actually proven pleasant, not just green._
194. **Make explicit build-vs-buy calls per platform component with a documented fallback ladder** `[S]` - For each leaf dependency the stack leans on (tray-icon, global-hotkey, keyring, wl-clipboard subprocess, ashpd portals, SQLCipher build), record now: use-as-is / wrap-thin / fork / hand-roll, plus the trigger that escalates one rung, e.g. 'global-hotkey: use-as-is on Win/mac, but on Wayland go straight to ashpd GlobalShortcuts; if portal unavailable, hand-roll is out-of-scope -> degrade'. _Value: The crate-maturity spike verifies versions but doesn't pre-decide what happens when a crate is found lacking mid-build, which is when schedule slips. A pre-agreed fallback ladder converts 'this crate doesn't do X' from a milestone-derailing investigation into a known, bounded move, and prevents accidentally adopting the wl-clipboard subprocess shortcut permanently._
195. **Reorder M5 to ship the privacy gate and capture-health indicator before snippets/transforms** `[S]` - Within M5, sequence the fail-closed gate wiring + visible capture-health state + default deny-list first and treat MVP snippets/transforms as the cuttable tail of that milestone, so if M5 runs long the thing that gets dropped is convenience features, never the privacy floor. _Value: M5 bundles the load-bearing privacy promise (Bet 3, 'provably private') with cuttable convenience (snippets, transforms) in one milestone with one exit gate. If schedule pressure forces a cut, the bundling makes it ambiguous what gives. Ordering privacy-first inside the milestone guarantees the differentiator survives and the snippet/transform set is the explicit, pre-agreed cut line._
